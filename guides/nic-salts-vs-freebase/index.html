Nic Salts vs Freebase Nicotine: What's the Difference?

Walking into a vape shop, you'll see e-liquids labelled "nic salt" and "freebase" - but what do they actually mean? This guide explains everything in plain English so you can choose the right type for your needs.

What is Freebase Nicotine?

Freebase nicotine is the "traditional" form of nicotine used in e-liquids since vaping began. It's pure nicotine that's been converted to its base form - the same method tobacco companies have used for decades to increase nicotine absorption in cigarettes.

üí® Freebase Characteristics

  • Stronger throat hit - Noticeable "kick" at the back of your throat
  • Slower absorption - Takes longer to feel the nicotine
  • Lower strengths - Usually 3mg, 6mg, or 12mg
  • Best for big clouds - Works great in high-power devices

Freebase nicotine becomes harsh at higher strengths (above 12mg), which is why traditional e-liquids max out around 18mg. This made it difficult for heavy smokers to get enough nicotine satisfaction from vaping.

What are Nic Salts?

Nicotine salts are a newer form of nicotine that occurs naturally in tobacco leaves. By adding benzoic acid, manufacturers can create e-liquids that deliver high nicotine (10-20mg) without the harsh throat hit.

üßÇ Nic Salt Characteristics

  • Smooth throat hit - Even at high strengths like 20mg
  • Faster absorption - Nicotine hits your system quicker
  • Higher strengths - Typically 10mg or 20mg in the UK
  • Less vapour - Better for discreet vaping

Nic salts were pioneered by JUUL in 2015 and revolutionised vaping for smokers. The faster nicotine delivery and smooth feel make it much easier to switch from cigarettes.

‚ö†Ô∏è Important Safety Note

Nic salts should only be used in low-power devices (under 20W). Using 20mg nic salts in a sub-ohm tank is dangerous - you'll consume far too much nicotine and feel very unwell.

Frequently Asked Questions

What are nic salts?

Nic salts (nicotine salts) are a form of nicotine that occurs naturally in tobacco leaves. They're combined with benzoic acid to create a smoother vape at higher nicotine strengths (10-20mg). Nic salts absorb faster into the bloodstream, providing satisfaction similar to smoking a cigarette.

Are nic salts better than freebase?

Neither is objectively "better" - they serve different purposes. Nic salts are better for heavy smokers, pod users, and those wanting quick nicotine satisfaction. Freebase is better for cloud chasers, sub-ohm vapers, and those who prefer lower nicotine with more vapour. Choose based on your needs.

Can I use nic salts in any device?

No! Nic salts should only be used in low-power devices like pod systems (under 20W). Using high-strength nic salts in sub-ohm devices is dangerous as you'll consume too much nicotine. Always check your device is suitable before using nic salts.

Why are nic salts smoother?

The addition of benzoic acid lowers the pH level of the nicotine, making it less alkaline. This chemical change makes the vapour much smoother on your throat, even at high strengths like 20mg where freebase would be unbearably harsh.

Are nic salts more addictive?

Nic salts deliver nicotine faster, which can make them feel more satisfying. However, this isn't necessarily "more addictive" - it simply mimics the nicotine delivery of cigarettes more closely. For people trying to quit smoking, this can actually be helpful.

What strength nic salt should I use?

In the UK, nic salts come in 10mg and 20mg. If you smoked 10+ cigarettes daily, start with 20mg. If you were a lighter smoker (under 10/day), try 10mg. You can always adjust based on how satisfied you feel.

Do nic salts use more e-liquid?

Generally no - because nic salts satisfy cravings faster, most people actually vape less frequently than with freebase. A 10ml bottle of 20mg nic salt often lasts longer than a 10ml bottle of 6mg freebase.
