Shortfills Explained: What Are They & How to Use Them

Shortfills offer the best value for experienced vapers, but they can be confusing at first. This guide explains everything you need to know about shortfills, nic shots, and how to mix them.

What is a Shortfill E-Liquid?

A shortfill is a bottle of nicotine-free e-liquid that isn't filled to the top. The "short fill" leaves room for you to add nicotine shots (nic shots) to reach your desired nicotine strength.

üì¶ Common Shortfill Sizes

  • 50ml in 60ml bottle - Space for 1 nic shot (10ml)
  • 100ml in 120ml bottle - Space for 2 nic shots (20ml)
  • 200ml in 250ml bottle - Space for 4-5 nic shots

The e-liquid itself contains 0mg nicotine - it's just VG, PG, and flavourings. You then add nicotine separately using nic shots.

Why Do Shortfills Exist?

Shortfills exist because of UK TPD (Tobacco Products Directive) regulations. The law states:

  • E-liquids containing nicotine can only be sold in 10ml bottles maximum
  • Maximum nicotine strength is 20mg/ml

This would make buying large quantities of nicotine e-liquid expensive and impractical. Shortfills are a legal workaround - you buy nicotine-free liquid in large bottles, then add nicotine yourself.

What are Nic Shots?

Nic shots (also called nicotine shots or nicotine boosters) are small 10ml bottles of unflavoured nicotine liquid. They typically come in 18mg/ml strength.

When you add a nic shot to a shortfill, the nicotine mixes with the flavoured e-liquid to create your final product.

üß™ Types of Nic Shots

  • Freebase (18mg) - Standard nic shots, slight throat hit
  • Salt Nic (18mg/20mg) - Smoother, faster absorption
  • 50/50 VG/PG - Balanced ratio
  • 70/30 VG/PG - For high-VG shortfills

Nic shots typically cost ¬£1-2 each. Some retailers include free nic shots when you buy shortfills.

How to Mix Shortfills

Mixing shortfills is simple - here's how:

1

Open the shortfill bottle

Remove the cap and nib (the plastic dripper part) from your shortfill bottle.

2

Add your nic shot(s)

Squeeze the entire contents of your nic shot into the shortfill bottle. The bottle should now be almost full.

3

Replace the cap

Put the nib and cap back on the bottle securely.

4

Shake thoroughly

Shake the bottle vigorously for 2-3 minutes. This ensures the nicotine is evenly distributed throughout the liquid.

5

Let it steep (optional)

For best results, leave the bottle for 24-48 hours before vaping. This allows the flavours to blend fully.

  • Huge flavour variety available
  • Choose your exact nicotine strength
  • Less plastic waste
  • Premium brands often only available as shortfills
  • Better for heavy vapers

  • Requires mixing (extra step)
  • Need to buy nic shots separately
  • Usually high VG (not for all devices)
  • Max practical strength is ~6mg
  • Not ideal for MTL/pod systems
  • Larger bottles less portable

Who Should Use Shortfills?

Shortfills are ideal if you:

  • Use a sub-ohm tank or RDA
  • Vape at 3mg or 6mg nicotine
  • Go through a lot of e-liquid
  • Want to save money long-term
  • Prefer high-VG for clouds

Shortfills are NOT ideal if you:

  • Use a pod system or MTL device
  • Need higher nicotine (10mg+)
  • Prefer 50/50 e-liquids
  • Don't want to mix yourself

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a shortfill e-liquid?

A shortfill is a larger bottle of nicotine-free e-liquid (usually 50ml or 100ml) with space left at the top to add nicotine shots. They're called "shortfills" because the bottle isn't filled to the top - this space is for adding nic shots.

How many nic shots do I need?

For 3mg strength: Add 1 nic shot per 50ml of shortfill. For 6mg strength: Add 2 nic shots per 50ml. So a 100ml shortfill needs 2 shots for 3mg or 4 shots for 6mg.

Can I use shortfills in a pod system?

Most shortfills are high-VG (70/30 or higher), which is too thick for pod systems. They're designed for sub-ohm tanks and RDAs. For pod systems, stick to 50/50 e-liquids or nic salts.

Do I have to add nicotine?

No! If you vape 0mg, you can use shortfills as they are without adding any nic shots. They're still great value compared to buying multiple 10ml bottles.

How long do shortfills last?

Unopened shortfills typically have a 2-year shelf life. Once opened and mixed with nicotine, use within 6-12 months for best flavour. Store in a cool, dark place away from sunlight.

What's the difference between shortfill and longfill?

Shortfills are nearly full bottles with space for nic shots. Longfills are concentrated flavour shots in small bottles that you add to a larger bottle of plain VG/PG base. Longfills require more DIY mixing.
