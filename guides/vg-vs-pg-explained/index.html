VG vs PG Explained: What's the Difference?

Every e-liquid contains VG and PG - but what do they actually do? This simple guide explains everything you need to know about VG/PG ratios and how to choose the right one for your device.

What is VG (Vegetable Glycerine)?

VG stands for Vegetable Glycerine - a thick, sweet, colourless liquid derived from vegetable oils (usually palm, coconut, or soy). It's used in many products including food, cosmetics, and medicines.

  • Produces big clouds - VG creates thick, dense vapour
  • Smooth throat hit - Very gentle on the throat
  • Slightly sweet - Adds subtle sweetness to flavours
  • Thick consistency - Can be slow to wick in small coils

If you've seen vapers blowing massive clouds, they're likely using high VG e-liquid (70% VG or higher). VG is the cloud-making ingredient.

What is PG (Propylene Glycol)?

PG stands for Propylene Glycol - a thin, odourless liquid that's been used safely in food, medicines, and cosmetics for decades. It's excellent at carrying flavour.

üí® PG Characteristics

  • Stronger throat hit - Simulates the feeling of smoking
  • Better flavour - Carries flavour more effectively
  • Less vapour - Produces thinner, less visible clouds
  • Thin consistency - Wicks quickly in any coil

If you're switching from smoking and want that "hit" at the back of your throat, higher PG e-liquids deliver this sensation better than VG.

Matching VG/PG Ratio to Your Device

Using the wrong ratio can cause problems - dry hits, burnt coils, or leaking. Here's a quick guide:

These devices have small coils that need thin liquid. High VG can cause dry hits and burn coils quickly. Nic salts work perfectly here.

Sub-ohm coils have bigger wicking holes that handle thicker liquid. 70/30 gives the best balance of clouds and flavour. Avoid high PG - it can leak.

Frequently Asked Questions

What does VG mean in vaping?

VG stands for Vegetable Glycerine - a thick, sweet liquid derived from vegetable oils. It produces large clouds of vapour and provides a smooth throat hit. High VG e-liquids (70%+) are best for sub-ohm devices and cloud chasing.

What does PG mean in vaping?

PG stands for Propylene Glycol - a thin liquid that carries flavour effectively and provides a stronger throat hit similar to smoking. High PG e-liquids (50%+) are best for pod systems, MTL devices, and those who want intense flavour.

Which VG/PG ratio is best for pod systems?

Pod systems work best with 50/50 VG/PG ratios or higher PG blends. The thinner liquid wicks properly in small coils. Nic salts typically use 50/50 ratios. Using high VG (70%+) in pods can cause dry hits and burnt coils.

Can I mix different VG/PG ratios?

Yes, you can mix e-liquids with different ratios together. This is common when adding nicotine shots to shortfills. Just be aware that mixing changes the final ratio - adding a 50/50 nic shot to a 70/30 shortfill will result in roughly 65/35.

Are VG and PG safe?

Both VG and PG have been used safely in food, medicine, and cosmetics for decades. They're approved for use by food safety authorities. While inhaling any substance carries some risk, VG and PG are considered much safer alternatives to tobacco smoke.

Why does high PG give a stronger throat hit?

PG is slightly irritating to the throat (in a good way for ex-smokers). This creates the "throat hit" sensation that mimics smoking. VG is much smoother and doesn't provide this sensation, which is why cloud chasers prefer it but some ex-smokers find it unsatisfying.
