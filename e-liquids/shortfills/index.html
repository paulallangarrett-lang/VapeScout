What Are Shortfill E-Liquids?

Shortfills are nicotine-free e-liquids sold in bottles larger than 10ml with space left to add your own nicotine shot. Due to UK TPD regulations, e-liquids containing nicotine can only be sold in 10ml bottles. Shortfills provide a legal workaround, allowing you to buy larger quantities and add nicotine yourself.

Common Shortfill Sizes

The most popular shortfill sizes are:

  • 50ml in 60ml bottle ‚Äì Add 1x 10ml nic shot for ~3mg strength
  • 100ml in 120ml bottle ‚Äì Add 2x 10ml nic shots for ~3mg strength
  • 200ml in 250ml bottle ‚Äì Add 4-5x 10ml nic shots for ~3mg strength

How to Add Nicotine

  1. Open your shortfill bottle
  2. Pour in the nicotine shot (usually 18mg strength)
  3. Replace the cap and shake vigorously for 1-2 minutes
  4. Let it steep for a few minutes before vaping

Who Are Shortfills For?

Shortfills are best suited for:

  • Sub-ohm vapers ‚Äì Using high-powered devices with low-resistance coils
  • Cloud chasers ‚Äì Looking for high VG e-liquids (70%+)
  • Cost-conscious vapers ‚Äì Better value per ml than 10ml bottles
  • Low nicotine users ‚Äì Those vaping 3-6mg nicotine

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a shortfill e-liquid?

A shortfill is a nicotine-free e-liquid sold in a bottle larger than 10ml, with space left to add a nicotine shot. For example, a 50ml shortfill comes in a 60ml bottle, leaving room for one 10ml nic shot. This is a workaround to TPD regulations which limit nicotine-containing e-liquids to 10ml bottles.

How do I add nicotine to a shortfill?

Simply pour a nicotine shot (usually 18mg) into the shortfill bottle, replace the cap, and shake well for 1-2 minutes. One 10ml nic shot in a 50ml shortfill gives approximately 3mg nicotine strength. For 6mg, add two nic shots (though you'll need a 50ml in a 70ml bottle or decant some liquid first).

Are shortfills suitable for pod systems?

Generally no. Shortfills are typically high VG (70VG or higher) which is too thick for pod systems and MTL devices. The thicker liquid can cause wicking issues and burnt coils. For pod systems, we recommend nic salt e-liquids which are 50/50 VG/PG and designed for lower-power devices.

Why are shortfills cheaper per ml?

Shortfills offer better value because you're buying in bulk and adding your own nicotine. The cost of nicotine shots (typically ¬£1-2 each) is much lower than the premium charged for pre-mixed nicotine e-liquids in 10ml bottles. A 50ml shortfill + nic shot often costs less than five 10ml bottles of the same e-liquid.

How long do shortfills last?

Unopened shortfills typically have a shelf life of 1-2 years from manufacture. Once opened and mixed with nicotine, we recommend using within 3-6 months for best flavour. Store in a cool, dark place away from direct sunlight. Nicotine can oxidise over time, which may darken the liquid and slightly alter the taste.
