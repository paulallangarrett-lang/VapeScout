What Are 50/50 E-Liquids?

50/50 e-liquids contain an equal ratio of Vegetable Glycerin (VG) and Propylene Glycol (PG). This balanced formula makes them ideal for mouth-to-lung (MTL) vaping, which mimics the draw of a traditional cigarette.

Understanding VG/PG Ratios

The two main ingredients in e-liquid serve different purposes:

  • VG (Vegetable Glycerin) ‚Äì Produces vapour clouds and adds sweetness. Higher VG = more vapour, smoother hit.
  • PG (Propylene Glycol) ‚Äì Carries flavour and provides throat hit. Higher PG = stronger throat sensation, thinner liquid.

A 50/50 ratio offers the best of both worlds: decent vapour production with a satisfying throat hit and excellent flavour delivery.

Nicotine Strengths Available

50/50 freebase e-liquids typically come in:

  • 3mg ‚Äì Light, for casual vapers or those stepping down
  • 6mg ‚Äì Medium-low, popular for light smokers
  • 12mg ‚Äì Medium, suits 10-15 cigarette/day smokers
  • 18mg ‚Äì High, for heavy smokers (20+ cigarettes/day)

Best Devices for 50/50 E-Liquids

50/50 e-liquids work best with:

  • Pod systems ‚Äì Caliburn, XROS, Argus (with MTL pods)
  • Pen-style starter kits ‚Äì Innokin Endura, Aspire PockeX
  • MTL tanks ‚Äì With coils rated 1.0 ohm or higher

üí° Tip: 50/50 for Beginners

If you're new to vaping and using a starter kit, 50/50 e-liquids are a great choice. They work reliably in most beginner devices and provide a familiar throat hit that many ex-smokers prefer.

Frequently Asked Questions

What does 50/50 e-liquid mean?

50/50 refers to the ratio of Vegetable Glycerin (VG) to Propylene Glycol (PG) in the e-liquid. A 50/50 mix provides balanced throat hit and vapour production, making it ideal for MTL (mouth-to-lung) vaping devices like pod systems and starter kits.

What's the difference between 50/50 and nic salts?

Both have similar VG/PG ratios suitable for pod systems, but they differ in nicotine type. 50/50 uses freebase nicotine which has a stronger throat hit at higher strengths. Nic salts use a smoother nicotine formula that's better absorbed, allowing higher strengths (10-20mg) without harshness. If you want a cigarette-like throat hit, choose 50/50. If you want smoothness with high nicotine, choose nic salts.

What devices work best with 50/50 e-liquids?

50/50 e-liquids work best with MTL (mouth-to-lung) devices including pod systems (Caliburn, XROS), pen-style vapes (Endura, PockeX), and starter kits with coils above 1.0 ohm. They're not recommended for sub-ohm tanks or high-powered devices, which work better with high-VG e-liquids.

What nicotine strength should I choose?

It depends on your smoking history:

  • Light smokers (1-10 cigarettes/day): 3-6mg
  • Average smokers (10-20 cigarettes/day): 6-12mg
  • Heavy smokers (20+ cigarettes/day): 12-18mg

If in doubt, start higher and reduce over time ‚Äì it's easier than struggling with cravings.

Can I use 50/50 in a sub-ohm tank?

It's not recommended. Sub-ohm tanks are designed for high-VG e-liquids (70VG+) and produce much more vapour. Using 50/50 in a sub-ohm tank at higher nicotine strengths can result in harsh throat hit and potentially too much nicotine. Stick to MTL devices for 50/50 e-liquids.

Looking for Smoother Nicotine?

Try nic salts for a smoother vaping experience with higher nicotine strengths.
