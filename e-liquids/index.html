E-Liquid FAQs

What is the difference between nic salts and freebase nicotine?

Nic salts (nicotine salts) provide a smoother throat hit at higher nicotine strengths, making them ideal for ex-smokers who need a stronger nicotine fix. Freebase nicotine (used in most shortfills and 50/50 e-liquids) delivers a more noticeable throat hit but can be harsh at higher strengths. Nic salts are absorbed faster by the body, more closely mimicking the experience of smoking a cigarette.

What nicotine strength should I choose?

20mg nic salts: Heavy smokers (20+ cigarettes/day) or those switching from disposable vapes
10mg nic salts: Moderate smokers (10-20 cigarettes/day)
3-6mg freebase: Light smokers or those stepping down
0mg: Those who've quit nicotine but still enjoy vaping

What is a shortfill e-liquid?

Shortfills are larger bottles (usually 50ml or 100ml) of nicotine-free e-liquid with space left for you to add a nicotine shot. Due to UK TPD regulations, nicotine-containing e-liquid can only be sold in 10ml bottles. Shortfills allow you to buy more e-liquid at once and add nicotine to your preferred strength. They offer better value per ml compared to 10ml bottles.

What does VG/PG ratio mean?

VG (Vegetable Glycerine): Produces more vapour, smoother throat hit, slightly sweet
PG (Propylene Glycol): Better flavour carrier, stronger throat hit, thinner consistency

70/30 VG/PG: Best for sub-ohm tanks and cloud production
50/50: Versatile, works in most devices
High PG (60%+): Better for pod systems and MTL vaping
